"content": "Students pursuing this B.S. degree must complete 3 hours of technical or scientific writing, choosing from ENG 3303 or ENG 3313, with a grade of C or higher required. No more than 3 credit hours may be applied to the student's major elective from any combination of: CS 3190 Cooperative Education (1 hour), CS 3290 Advanced Cooperative Education (2 hours), CS 4100 Computer Science Internship (1 hour), CS 4298 Undergraduate Research I (2 hours), CS 4299 Undergraduate Research II (2 hours), CS 4395 Independent Study in Computer Science (3 hours), HON 4390B Honors Capstone (department approval required, 3 hours), or RES 4399 Mentored Research and Creative Expression (department approval required, 3 hours). The required courses for this major include 14 of the 20 hours of coursework required for a Mathematics or Applied Mathematics minor, so the degree plan includes two additional courses needed to complete one of these minors. Nine hours of writing intensive (WI) courses are required for graduation. Students must complete a minimum of 36 advanced hours (3000 or 4000 level courses). If two years of the same language were taken in high school, no additional language hours are required; otherwise two semesters of the same modern language (1410 and 1420) must be taken at the college level. Students must complete 46 hours of Computer Science courses, including one CS project course from CS 4318, CS 4326, CS 4380, or CS 4398. Students entering Texas State with fewer than 16 credit hours completed after high school graduation are required to take US 1100; all others are exempt. Students may need an additional elective to reach the 120 minimum total credit hour requirement."
